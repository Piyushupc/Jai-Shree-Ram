About Lord Ram

Lord Ram, the seventh incarnation of Lord Vishnu, is the epitome of righteousness, devotion, and virtue. He is the central figure of the Ramayana, an epic that teaches us the values of Dharma, truth, and devotion.

Born to King Dasharatha and Queen Kaushalya in Ayodhya, Lord Ram is worshipped as Maryada Purushottam— the ideal man who upheld truth and duty in every aspect of his life.

Key Teachings from Ramayana:

  • Righteousness and Dharma are the ultimate duties.
  • Respect and devotion to parents and elders are paramount.
  • Compassion and truth always lead to victory.
  • Friendship and loyalty are the greatest virtues.
